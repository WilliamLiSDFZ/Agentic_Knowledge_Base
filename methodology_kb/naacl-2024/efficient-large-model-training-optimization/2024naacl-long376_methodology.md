# Mind’s Mirror: Distilling Self-Evaluation Capability and Comprehensive Thinking from Large Language Models

**Source**: https://aclanthology.org/2024.naacl-long.376/

## [POSITIVE] Self-Evaluation Capability Distillation
Distilling the LLM's ability to evaluate the correctness of its own chain-of-thought reasoning into the SLM, enabling it to distinguish correct from incorrect reasoning.

**Delta**: Outperforms baseline CoT distillation; e.g., +3.8% on SVAMP pseudo-labels (55.5 vs 51.7), +2.8% on SVAMP human-labels (67.8 vs 65.0)
**Condition**: Applied when training SLMs to mitigate flawed reasoning and hallucinations inherited from LLMs; effective across all three tasks (SVAMP, CQA, ANLI).

**Evidence**: "By learning the ability of LLMs to analyze right from wrong, SLMs can understand both what should and should not be generated, enhancing their predictive accuracy and reliability in various NLP tasks."

## [POSITIVE] Multiple CoT Distillation (Diverse CoTs)
Using multiple distinct chain-of-thought reasoning paths generated by the LLM (instead of a single CoT) to train the SLM, providing a broader range of reasoning processes.

**Delta**: 5 CoTs vs 1 CoT: +3.1% on SVAMP pseudo-labels (54.8 vs 51.7), +3.7% on SVAMP human-labels (68.7 vs 65.0)
**Condition**: Used in step 4 of training; effective across all tasks, with diminishing returns beyond 5 CoTs (performance degradation observed at 7+ CoTs).

**Evidence**: "A single instance of CoT might not capture the diverse reasoning routes LLMs can explore, limiting the richness of the distilled knowledge of SLMs."

## [POSITIVE] Multiple Self-Evaluation Outputs per CoT
Generating multiple self-evaluation outputs for each CoT to distill a more comprehensive self-evaluation capability into the SLM.

**Delta**: Increasing self-evaluation outputs from 0 to 5 per CoT improves accuracy; e.g., with 5 CoTs on SVAMP human-labels: 68.7 (0 eval) → 72.7 (5 evals)
**Condition**: Applied in step 3 of training; works best when combined with multiple CoTs (5 CoTs).

**Evidence**: "As the number of self-evaluation outputs per CoT increases, there is a notable enhancement in the accuracy of the distilled SLMs... underscoring the synergistic effect of combining multiple CoTs with corresponding self-evaluation."

## [POSITIVE] Multi-Task Learning with Task Prefixes
Training the SLM to predict both labels and rationales using separate task prefixes ('predict:' for labels, 'explain:' for rationales) in a multi-task loss function.

**Delta**: Outperforms treating rationale and label predictions as a single joint task (as per Hsieh et al. 2023); reduces computation overhead during inference.
**Condition**: Used in both self-evaluation training (step 3) and CoT reasoning training (step 4).

**Evidence**: "Hsieh et al. (2023) have demonstrated that multi-task learning can lead to better performance than simply treating rationale and label predictions as a single joint task, and can reduce computation overhead during inference."

## [POSITIVE] Hyperparameter λ for Loss Weighting
A hyperparameter λ that balances the cross-entropy loss between rationale prediction and label prediction in the multi-task loss function.

**Delta**: Optimal λ=0.5 yields best performance; e.g., on SVAMP human-labels with 5 CoTs w/ self-evaluation: 72.7% at λ=0.5 vs lower values at other λ.
**Condition**: Applied in both loss functions (self-evaluation and CoT reasoning); optimal value determined empirically (λ=0.5).

**Evidence**: "The best way to enhance model performance is to learn high-quality labels and rationales in a balanced way."

## [POSITIVE] Few-Shot CoT Prompting for LLM Generation
Using few-shot chain-of-thought prompts (5-shot) to guide the LLM in generating high-quality, standardized CoTs and self-evaluation outputs.

**Delta**: Enables consistent format and quality; foundational for all subsequent distillation steps.
**Condition**: Used in steps 1 and 2 for generating teacher data from the LLM.

**Evidence**: "We utilize few-shot CoT prompting to enhance the quality and standardize the formats of the CoTs generated by the LLM."

## [NEUTRAL] Optional Data Quality Filtering
Removing duplicate and low-quality CoTs and self-evaluation outputs from the LLM-generated data before training.

**Delta**: Described as optional; no quantitative delta reported.
**Condition**: Optional preprocessing step; not used in main experiments.

**Evidence**: "We suggest examining the quality and diversity of multiple CoTs and self-evaluation outputs generated by the LLM for the same input, and removing duplicates and outputs of inferior quality, to enhance data quality. This is an optional step."

## [NEUTRAL] Using Pseudo-Labels vs Human-Annotated Labels
Training the SLM using either LLM-generated pseudo-labels or human-annotated labels as the target labels.

**Delta**: Human-labels consistently outperform pseudo-labels (e.g., SVAMP 5 CoTs w/ self-evaluation: 72.7% vs 60.3%), but the method's relative improvement over baselines is robust to label quality.
**Condition**: Used in all experiments; pseudo-labels used when human labels are unavailable.

**Evidence**: "Regardless of the type of training labels used, our method exhibits consistent advantages, suggesting that the benefits of our distillation method are also robust to variations in label quality."
