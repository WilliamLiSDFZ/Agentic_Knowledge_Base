# TopicGPT: A Prompt-based Topic Modeling Framework

**Source**: https://aclanthology.org/2024.naacl-long.164/

## [POSITIVE] Prompt-based topic generation with iterative refinement
Iteratively prompting an LLM to generate new topics given a sample of documents and a list of previously generated topics, then refining by merging near-duplicates and removing infrequent topics.

**Delta**: Harmonic mean purity improves from 0.64 (LDA) to 0.74 (TopicGPT) on Wiki; misaligned topics reduced from 62.4% (LDA) to 30.3% (TopicGPT refined) on Wiki.
**Condition**: Applicable when generating topics from a text corpus; requires a sample of documents and a small set of example topics.

**Evidence**: "TopicGPT achieves a harmonic mean purity of 0.74 against human-annotated Wikipedia topics compared to 0.64 for the strongest baseline."

## [POSITIVE] Natural language topic labels and descriptions
Representing topics as concise natural language labels paired with broad one-sentence descriptions, instead of bags of words.

**Delta**: 30.3% of TopicGPT's topics are misaligned compared to 62.4% for LDA on Wikipedia.
**Condition**: Used in all TopicGPT experiments; improves interpretability and alignment with human judgment.

**Evidence**: "Topics generated by TopicGPT include natural language labels and descriptions that make them immediately interpretable without needing a separate labeling step."

## [POSITIVE] Topic assignment with quoted evidence
Assigning documents to topics by prompting the LLM to output a topic label, a document-specific description, and a supporting quote from the document.

**Delta**: Outperforms baselines on all metrics; e.g., purity 0.74 vs 0.64 (LDA) on Wiki.
**Condition**: Used in the assignment stage; requires the generated topic list and a document.

**Evidence**: "The quoted text improves the verifiability of TopicGPT's assignments, which has been a long-standing concern with traditional methods such as LDA."

## [POSITIVE] Self-correction for topic assignments
Parsing invalid or hallucinated assignments (e.g., 'None'/'Error') and re-prompting the LLM to reassign a valid topic.

**Delta**: Resolves all topic hallucinations and formatting issues within a retry limit of 10.
**Condition**: Applied during topic assignment when the parser detects invalid outputs.

**Evidence**: "We enable self-correction with a retry limit of 10 and end up resolving all topic hallucinations and formatting issues within this limit."

## [POSITIVE] Using GPT-4 for topic generation and GPT-3.5-turbo for assignment
Using a stronger LLM (GPT-4) for the complex topic generation task and a cheaper LLM (GPT-3.5-turbo) for the simpler assignment task.

**Delta**: TopicGPT achieves best performance; Mistral-7B for assignment still outperforms baselines but with ~6 point purity drop.
**Condition**: Default setting; recommended when budget allows for closed-source APIs.

**Evidence**: "Most of our experiments implement topic generation with GPT-4 and topic assignment with GPT-3.5-turbo, which costs around $100 per dataset."

## [POSITIVE] Sampling documents for topic generation
Using a uniformly random subset of documents (e.g., 1,000-1,100) for topic generation instead of the full corpus to reduce cost.

**Delta**: Number of new topics plateaus after ~600 documents; no significant loss in coverage.
**Condition**: Applicable when corpus is large; sample size can be chosen based on budget or until topic generation plateaus.

**Evidence**: "In both datasets, the number of new topics that remain after refinement plateaus after this threshold - after around 600 documents have been generated."

## [POSITIVE] Refinement: merging near-duplicate topics via embeddings and LLM
Using Sentence-Transformer embeddings to identify topic pairs with cosine similarity ≥ 0.5, then prompting the LLM to merge near-duplicate pairs.

**Delta**: Misaligned topics reduced from 39.8% (unrefined) to 30.3% (refined) on Wiki; repeated topics reduced from 1.1% to 0.0%.
**Condition**: Applied after initial topic generation; threshold can be adjusted.

**Evidence**: "Refinement consistently reduces the number of out-of-scope and repeated topics."

## [POSITIVE] Refinement: removing infrequent topics
Removing topics that occur below a frequency threshold (e.g., 10 for Bills, 5 for Wiki) to eliminate minor or spurious topics.

**Delta**: Reduces out-of-scope topics; e.g., on Bills from 65.0% (unrefined) to 27.8% (refined).
**Condition**: Applied after merging; threshold should be tuned to avoid removing important topics.

**Evidence**: "Refinement consistently reduces the number of out-of-scope and repeated topics."

## [POSITIVE] Using a small number of example topics (2-3)
Providing only 2-3 manually written example topics as few-shot demonstrations for topic generation.

**Delta**: Additional example topics (5 total) led to worst performance: purity 0.50 vs 0.57 (default).
**Condition**: Recommended for best results; too many examples may overwhelm the model.

**Evidence**: "The setting with additional example topics obtains the worst performance, suggesting that simply adding more example topics is not always helpful."

## [POSITIVE] Multi-label assignment prompt
Allowing the LLM to assign multiple topics per document instead of a single topic.

**Delta**: Three out of five Wiki examples were assigned to the ground truth as well as the originally assigned topic when using multi-label prompt.
**Condition**: Recommended when documents may cover multiple topics; improves recall of relevant topics.

**Evidence**: "With this updated prompt, three out of the five Wiki examples are assigned to the ground truth as well as the originally assigned topic."

## [NEUTRAL] Using Mistral-7B-Instruct for topic assignment
Using the open-source Mistral-7B-Instruct model as a lower-cost alternative for topic assignment.

**Delta**: Absolute purity decrease of about ~6 points compared to GPT-3.5-turbo, but still outperforms baselines on P1 and ARI.
**Condition**: Applicable when cost is a concern; not as effective as GPT-3.5-turbo but still viable.

**Evidence**: "Mistral's topic assignments align reasonably well with human ground truth, though not to the same degree as GPT-3.5-turbo; the bottom row of Table 1 shows an absolute purity decrease of about ~6 points."

## [NEGATIVE] Using Mistral-7B-Instruct for topic generation
Attempting to use Mistral-7B-Instruct or GPT-3.5-turbo for the topic generation stage.

**Delta**: Mistral produced 1,418 topics (vs GPT-4's manageable number); most were overly specific and would be removed during refinement.
**Condition**: Not recommended; only GPT-4 or similarly capable models should be used for topic generation.

**Evidence**: "Both models struggle to follow formatting instructions for topic generation. In total, Mistral and GPT-3.5-turbo produced 1,418 and 151 topics, respectively."

## [NEUTRAL] Out-of-domain prompts
Using prompts written for one dataset (Wiki) on another dataset (Bills) to test robustness.

**Delta**: Purity 0.55 vs 0.57 (default); still outperforms baselines.
**Condition**: Demonstrates robustness; applicable when transferring prompts across domains.

**Evidence**: "TopicGPT produces comparable topical alignment with the ground truth across all modified experimental settings for the Bills data."

## [NEUTRAL] Shuffling sampled documents for topic generation
Shuffling the order of documents in the generation sample to test sensitivity to document order.

**Delta**: Purity 0.55 vs 0.57 (default); comparable performance.
**Condition**: Shows robustness to document order; applicable in any setting.

**Evidence**: "TopicGPT produces comparable topical alignment with the ground truth across all modified experimental settings."

## [NEUTRAL] Using a different generation sample
Applying TopicGPT to a different random sample of documents for topic generation.

**Delta**: Purity 0.57 vs 0.57 (default); identical performance.
**Condition**: Demonstrates robustness to sampling variation.

**Evidence**: "TopicGPT produces comparable topical alignment with the ground truth across all modified experimental settings."
