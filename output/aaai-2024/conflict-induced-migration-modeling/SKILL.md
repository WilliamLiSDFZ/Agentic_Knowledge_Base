# Conflict Induced Migration Modeling

This skill covers developing and applying computational agent-based models (ABMs) to simulate forced migration patterns, specifically by formalizing theoretical drivers (e.g., violence, social networks, resource scarcity) into rules that govern the decision-making and movement of autonomous agents. Its core application is to generate and test mechanistic explanations for displacement dynamics, enabling scenario analysis and policy evaluation in conflict settings.
