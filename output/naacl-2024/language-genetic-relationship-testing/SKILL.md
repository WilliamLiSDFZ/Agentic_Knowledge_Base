# Language Genetic Relationship Testing

This skill involves applying statistical methods, particularly likelihood ratio tests, to evaluate hypotheses of genealogical relatedness between languages by comparing probabilistic models of lexical or phonological data. It is used to quantify the strength of evidence for or against proposed language families and to distinguish shared inheritance from chance resemblance or borrowing.
