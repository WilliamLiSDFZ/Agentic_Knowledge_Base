# Modeling Empathetic Alignment in Conversation

**Source**: [https://aclanthology.org/2024.naacl-long.172/](https://aclanthology.org/2024.naacl-long.172/)

**TLDR**: It introduces a new method to model empathetic alignment in dialogue by explicitly reasoning about and communicating understanding of another's experience.

## Abstract

AbstractEmpathy requires perspective-taking: empathetic responses require a person to reason about what another has experienced and communicate that understanding in language. However, most NLP approaches to empathy do not explicitly model this alignment process. Here, we introduce a new approach to recognizing alignment in empathetic speech, grounded in Appraisal Theory. We introduce a new dataset of over 9.2K span-level annotations of different types of appraisals of a person’s experience and over 3K empathetic alignments between a speaker’s and observer’s speech. Through computational experiments, we show that these appraisals and alignments can be accurately recognized. In experiments in over 9.2M Reddit conversations, we find that appraisals capture meaningful groupings of behavior but that most responses have minimal alignment. However, we find that mental health professionals engage with substantially more empathetic alignment.
